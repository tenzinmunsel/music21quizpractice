Complete Music Terminology Quiz

1. What is a strong, regular, repeated pattern of sound and silence?

Correct Answer: Rhythm

2. What is the term for the speed at which a piece of music is played?

Correct Answer: Tempo

3. What is the fundamental, steady pulse that forms the unit of time in music?

Correct Answer: Beat

4. Which note gets one beat of SOUND?

Correct Answer: Quarter Note

5. Which rest gets one beat of SILENCE?

Correct Answer: Quarter Rest

6. Which note gets half a beat of SOUND?

Correct Answer: Eighth Note

7. Which rest gets half a beat of SILENCE?

Correct Answer: Eighth Rest

8. What is a note that is played with more emphasis?

Correct Answer: Accented Note

9. What is a note that is played with less emphasis?

Correct Answer: Tap

10. What is a classification system that categorizes music into different styles?

Correct Answer: Genre

11. What is a smaller, more specific category within a larger genre?

Correct Answer: Subgenre

12. The particular characteristics of a piece of music (melody, harmony, etc.) are its:

Correct Answer: Style

13. Who writes and arranges original music in a variety of musical styles?

Correct Answer: Composer

14. Who creates the melody and writes the lyrics?

Correct Answer: Songwriter

15. What family of instruments produces sound when struck, shaken, or scraped?

Correct Answer: Percussion

16. Which drum is large, produces a low-pitched sound, and is often played with a foot pedal?

Correct Answer: Bass/Kick Drum

17. Which drum has wires (snares) stretched across the bottom head for a sharp sound?

Correct Answer: Snare Drum

18. Which instrument consists of a pair of cymbals mounted on a stand?

Correct Answer: Hi-hat Cymbal

19. What is the correct sticking pattern for a single stroke?

Correct Answer: RLRL RLRL

20. What is the correct sticking pattern for a double stroke?

Correct Answer: RRLL RRLL

21. What is the correct sticking pattern for a paradiddle?

Correct Answer: RLRR LRLL
